Emergent temporal correlations in self-regulated networks

Framework: Neural population dynamics viewed as a branching process
  • time step $\Delta t$ with neuron state $a_{t,j}\in\{0,1\}$
  • external excitation: rate $h$
  • internal excitation: connectivity matrix $w_{ij}$
  • spiking neuron excites on average $m=f(w_{ij})$ further neurons (branching parameter)
  • key parameters:
    • $h$ strength of external input
    • $m$ strength of internal activation

The corresponding branching process:

$\mathbb{E}(A_{t+1}|A_t) = m A_t + hN\Delta t \hspace{3em}$

with

$\overline{a} = \frac{A}{N} = \frac{h \Delta t}{1-m}$

$\overline{a} = \frac{A}{N} = \frac{h \Delta t}{1-m}$

Henkel, Hinrichsen & Lübeck, "Non-Equilibrium Phase Transitions" (2008)
Zierenberg, Wilting & Priesemann, Phys. Rev. X (2018)

Emergent temporal correlations in self-regulated networks

Homeostatic self-regulation:

post-synaptic weight $\alpha_j$
We treat this as a generic negative feedback with target rate $r^\ast$ and timescale $\tau_\mathrm{hp}$:

$\Delta\alpha_j = (\Delta t~r^\ast - a_j)\frac{\Delta t}{\tau_\mathrm{hp}} \qquad\text{with}\qquad \alpha_j\geq0$

such that the mean activity $\overline{a}$

$\overline{a} \to \Delta t~r^\ast$

$\overline{a} \to \Delta t~r^\ast$

Equating both results, we obtain for the branching parameter

$m = 1-h/r^\ast$

The branching parameter $m$ is directly related to an exponential autocorrelation time

$\tau = -\Delta t/\ln(m) = -\Delta t/\ln(1-h/r^\ast)$
