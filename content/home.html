I am a theoretical physicist now in Neuroscience, fascinated by the question of how the brain may work. The brain is composed of many different areas, with quite diverse functions. In the cerebral cortex, these areas are mostly composed of recurrent neural networks, which integrate different input signals together with their past state in order to perform generic computations. I want to understand such generic computations by using analytical and numerical tools from statistical physics and dynamical systems theory. In particular, I study how computations may be realized by the collective dynamics of recurrent neural networks, how they depend on network topology, and how they rely on emergent correlations that arise in the vicinity of non-equilibrium phase transitions. Insights form this bottom-up approach may shed light on why our brain is structured the way it is, and may help to guide the construction of optimized architectures for artificial neural networks.
